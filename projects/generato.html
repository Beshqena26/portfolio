Case Study

Designing an AI Image Generator for Everyone

Role Product & UI/UX Designer
Timeline 4 weeks
Team Size Solo
Tools Figma, Midjourney, FigJam

Hey there 👋 Hope your day's going great.
Let's travel through my design journey.

My mission?

Redesigning the entire experience for an AI-powered image generator to make it more intuitive, accessible, and enjoyable for everyday users. The goal was simple — help anyone create stunning images without needing to learn complex prompt engineering or technical jargon.

The Research Saga Begins

I started by doing what any curious designer would do — analyzing what's already out there.

01

Competitive analysis across Midjourney, DALL-E, Stable Diffusion, and Leonardo AI

02

User interviews with 12 participants ranging from beginners to power users

03

Behavioral analysis using heatmaps, recordings, and analytics data

Midjourney
DALL-E
Stable Diffusion
Leonardo AI

And you know what?

Competitive tools were — overwhelming, confusing, full of jargon.

Prompt engineering — you needed to be a literal scientist to get good results.

That was my Aha! moment.

I realized the fundamental problem wasn't the AI models themselves — it was how they were presented to users. The interface was designed for engineers, not for everyday creative people who just want beautiful results.

The interface itself was the barrier, not the technology.

The Loop of Doom

After organizing data from the design process, I identified the core pain points in the user journey.

And then... I got stuck. Like, really stuck.

How do you make something as complex as prompt engineering feel simple without dumbing it down? How do you give users creative control without overwhelming them?

?

How do you balance simplicity with creative control?

?

How do you help users who don't know what they want?

?

What if the first result isn't what they had in mind?

Our first try? Auto-correction.

We built an AI assistant that would auto-correct and auto-enhance user prompts in real-time. The idea was to take whatever users typed and silently make it better.

But it felt like the tool was overriding user intent. People didn't trust results they didn't control.

Back to the drawing board.

This led to a fundamental shift in our design strategy.

Dynamic Prompt Enhancement

A side drawer, a live sidebar that suggests improvements to the user's prompt in real-time — without overriding their intent. Users choose what to accept.

Prompt Strength Indicator

A real-time visual signal (like a password strength meter) that shows how detailed and effective the current prompt is, encouraging users to add more context.

Results?

After 3 rounds of usability testing and iterating, here's what we saw.

+40% User satisfaction
-60% Prompt retry rate
3x Faster first result

Minimalism Over Chaos

Every element on screen earns its place. No feature bloat, no visual noise. Reduce cognitive load at every touchpoint.

Clean, decluttered layouts.

White space isn't wasted space — it's breathing room. The interface stays calm so users can focus on what matters: their creativity.

Contextual tools instead of overwhelming menus.

Controls appear when needed, where needed. Progressive disclosure keeps the interface simple for beginners while remaining powerful for experts.

A customizable theme system for accessibility and personal comfort.

Dark mode, light mode, and high contrast options. Because a tool you use for hours needs to feel like home.

No more "spaceship cockpit" UI.
Just a calm, modern experience anyone could enjoy.

Final Outcome

The redesigned Generato became an AI-powered creative tool that feels approachable, empowering, and delightful. By focusing on user intent over technical complexity, we transformed a specialist tool into something anyone can pick up and create with.

92% Task completion rate
4.8 User satisfaction (out of 5)
