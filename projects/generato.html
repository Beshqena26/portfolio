Twenty-four stories came out of the interviews, but most of them were features, not problems. I kept cutting until three stayed — the three that mapped directly back to the research themes.

Interview verbatim · P04

"I kept pressing generate hoping it would just figure out what I meant. By the fourth try I realized the tool wasn't going to meet me halfway."

— Returning user, churned session 2
Designer note

The emotional arc was the unlock. Users weren't confused — they were optimistic, then slowly abandoned. The drop happens between Try and Fail, not at the start.

That shifted the redesign target from "onboarding" to "the second attempt." Most tools fix the first run. I had to fix the one after.

Target: the second attempt

Designer note

The audit showed Generate sitting as one of eight peer items in the top bar. Visually, it read like Settings or Help. No wonder new users skimmed past it.

v2 tried to tuck half the siblings into a Browse hub. v3 won the card sort — Generate became the spine, everything else a satellite.

One hero, three satellites

Designer note

v1 mirrored the old flow but politer. Testers still got lost at step 4 — "enhancement" felt like a second job.

v2 added a template picker up front. Faster, but users reported feeling "boxed in." v3 collapses enhancement into the prompt field itself. No new screens.

Fewer screens. More trust.

Why Conversational failed

Chat framing made every run feel like a negotiation. Users wanted a canvas, not a conversation partner.

02
Why Visual Builder failed

Ten controls on screen tested worse than the audit baseline. Choice paralysis beat the old interface overload.

03
Why Smart Enhancement won

One prompt field + an inline strength meter. It earned trust by being quiet, not clever — the exact opposite of v1.
