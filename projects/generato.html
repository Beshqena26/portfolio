Context & goals

AI image generation exploded — but shipping a usable visual still meant a relay race: generate in one tool, remove the background in a second, upscale in a third, erase artifacts in a fourth. Generato's bet: merge the whole pipeline into one workspace that a first-time user can operate with zero training, without dumbing it down for professionals.

  • Business: a compelling MVP fast, in a market where competitors ship weekly.
  • Users: from advanced designers to first-time creators — one interface for both.
  • Quality bar: outputs must be production-ready — campaigns, e-commerce, print.

The gap: an accessible, end-to-end platform where generation, editing and enhancement live in one flow. That gap became Generato's mission.

Research & who I designed for

Interviews and surveys with digital creators, social media managers, e-commerce sellers and agencies; task observation of their ideation-to-asset flows; and sentiment mining across Reddit, Discord, Dribbble and Behance. Four patterns kept repeating — speed beats perfection, low tolerance for complexity, a craving for one cohesive flow, and fear of pixelated results at print size. They condensed into three personas:

The defining decision: canvas-first, not prompt-first

Low-fi wireframes tested two competing layouts. This one decision shaped the entire product:

✓ Canvas-first

The workspace opens on an evolving canvas where generation and editing merge. Users see and edit in the same place — the disconnect between "generator" and "editor" disappears. Validated through rapid prototype testing: more creative control without feeling technical.

✕ Prompt-first

Prioritized the text input and results feed — familiar from MidJourney, but it reinforces the exact fragmentation we were killing: generate here, edit somewhere else. Testers treated results as endpoints instead of starting points.

Progressive disclosure

Beginners see a prompt bar and quick-setting chips (aspect, resolution, style, lighting, composition). Power users expand panels for depth. One interface serves both personas — no "pro mode" fork.

Prompt strength checker

Research showed most users can't write effective prompts. A built-in checker coaches them toward better results — a small feature that bridges beginners to professional output quality.

The workspace

Dark canvas, one electric-lime accent, Clash Grotesk display type. The sidebar holds the five tools; credits and upgrade sit bottom-left; every setting is a chip on the prompt bar, not a buried panel. Empty states illustrate instead of lecture.

Type system

Results

  • 92% of first-time users generated and refined an image within 5 minutes, unaided. Testers called it "effortless" and "a tool I'd actually use daily."
  • ~40% time savings reported by marketers versus their previous 3–4-tool pipeline; agencies noted it could replace 2–3 subscriptions.
  • 3 tools per session on average — the integration bet worked: people don't just generate, they finish.
  • Designers singled out the upscaler and Magic Eraser as what made AI output production-ready.

"Not just generation like MidJourney, not just editing like Photoshop — an end-to-end ecosystem balancing speed, quality, and accessibility."

What I'd do differently

The hardest trade-off was depth versus simplicity — progressive disclosure solved most of it, but only after several iterations that earlier user testing would have shortened. And some users initially distrusted AI outputs entirely; positioning Generato as a creative partner rather than a replacement should have been baked into the UI copy from day one, not added later.
